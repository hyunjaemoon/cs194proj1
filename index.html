Images of the Russian Empire: Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii photo collection

By Hyun Jae Moon

Introduction

The goal of this project is to extract the three color channel images (R, G, B), place them on top of each other with an appropriate alignment using both exhaustive and pyramid implementation.

Exhaustive Method

For jpg files, which are lot smaller in file size, I've implemented an exhaustive algorithm to detect pixel offsets and align the three color channels appropriately. I would first align green channel to blue channel and find the appropriate pixel offset of the green channel. Then, I would do the same for red channel to blue channel. Finally, I would stack all three channels into one colored image. Here are the results for the jpg files after alignment with their respective offsets.

cathedral.jpg | G:(-1, 1), R:(-1, 7)

monastery.jpg | G:(0, -6), R:(1, 9)

tobolsk.jpg | G:(2, 3), R:(3, 6)

Pyramid Method

The downside of the exhaustive method is that it takes too much time to process large image files such as tiff files. To fasten the process, we must rescale the image to a smaller pixel margins, such that alignment can be processed at a much faster rate. I've continuously rescaled the image by halving per step and check the image size until it is smaller than 65536 (256 * 256). Then, we would perform the exhaustive method on a smaller image, and exponentiate the offsets by the number of times I have halved the images. Then it will produce the correct offset.

cathedral.jpg | G:(-1, 1), R:(-1, 7)

monastery.jpg | G:(0, -6), R:(1, 9)

tobolsk.jpg | G:(2, 3), R:(3, 6)
