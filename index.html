Images of the Russian Empire: Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii photo collection

By Hyun Jae Moon

Introduction

The goal of this project is to extract the three color channel images (R, G, B), place them on top of each other with an appropriate alignment using both exhaustive and pyramid implementation.

Exhaustive Method

For jpg files, which are lot smaller in file size, I've implemented an exhaustive algorithm to detect pixel offsets and align the three color channels appropriately. The exhuastive algorithm would check in the range of [-15, 15] pixels, and search for the largest Nomralized Correlation. I would first align green channel to blue channel and find the appropriate pixel offset of the green channel. Then, I would do the same for red channel to blue channel. Finally, I would stack all three channels into one colored image. Here are the results for the jpg files after alignment with their respective offsets.

cathedral.jpg | G:(-1, 1), R:(-1, 7)

monastery.jpg | G:(0, -6), R:(1, 9)

tobolsk.jpg | G:(2, 3), R:(3, 6)

Pyramid Method

The downside of the exhaustive method is that it takes too much time to process large image files such as tiff files. To fasten the process, we must rescale the image to a smaller pixel margins, such that alignment can be processed at a much faster rate. I've continuously rescaled the image by 1/3 per step and proceed 4 times, which would result in 81th of the original image size. Then, we would perform the exhaustive method on a smaller image, and check for the range of [offset * 3 ± 2]. Then it will produce the correct offset. I have originally attempted halving the image for 5 steps, but the parameters I mentioned produced a better image result.
